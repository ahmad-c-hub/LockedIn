🧬 The Science

Your Brain Can Physically Change

Neuroplasticity the brain's ability to rewire itself through experience is the biological foundation of all cognitive training. When you repeatedly challenge a specific cognitive function, the relevant neural circuits grow denser synaptic connections and myelinate (speed up) their axons.

Research by Klingberg (2010) and Jaeggi et al. (2008) demonstrated that targeted working memory training produces structural brain changes visible on MRI and transfers to untrained tasks, a phenomenon called far transfer.

✅ Why It Works

Four Pillars of Cognitive Growth

LockedIn's effectiveness rests on four well-established principles from cognitive and behavioral science.

01

Deliberate Practice

Ericsson's research (1993) shows that improvement requires training at the edge of current ability. LockedIn's adaptive difficulty system ensures you're always working in the optimal challenge zone.

02

Spaced Repetition

Daily short sessions exploit the spacing effect the finding that distributed practice produces stronger long-term memory encoding than massed practice (Cepeda et al., 2006).

03

Dual Coding

Games combine visual and spatial information, engaging multiple memory systems simultaneously. Paivio (1986) showed this dual encoding dramatically strengthens recall and transfer.

04

Behavioral Consistency

Habit formation research (Lally et al., 2010) shows it takes 66 days on average to automate a behavior. LockedIn's habit tracker and streak system are built specifically to get you to that milestone.
